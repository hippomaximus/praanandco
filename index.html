First principles

Cigar smoke is not one substance, but two — and each calls for a different remedy.

Step into a room an hour after a cigar has been put out. The visible smoke has gone; the scent has not. That residual gap is the entire deficiency of single-stage purification, and the reason most rooms one walks into smell of yesterday's tobacco rather than today's.

i — Particulate matter

What one sees

Mechanical pollutant

Visible smoke is an aerosol of fine particles — tar droplets, ash, condensed combustion products. The bulk fall between 0.1 and 1 micron in diameter. They settle on fabric, hang in the air, and obscure the room.

  • Tar and nicotine droplets suspended in air
  • Ash and combustion particulates
  • Captured efficiently by HEPA-grade mechanical filtration
  • Removed only as air passes through the device
ii — Gas-phase compounds

What one smells

Molecular pollutant

The signature scent of cigar is gas-phase — pyridines, ammonia, phenols, aldehydes, and dozens of further volatile organic compounds. They pass through filter media as though it were not there, and embed themselves in fabric, leather, and panelling.

  • Hundreds of distinct VOCs from tobacco combustion
  • Molecules far smaller than any mechanical filter mesh
  • Activated carbon adsorbs a portion, then saturates
  • Only molecular-level intervention truly addresses them
The Praan & Co. approach

Two stages, working in concert.

Mechanical filtration handles what filters do well. Bipolar plasma handles what filters cannot. Neither is sufficient alone; the combination is the whole of the engineering case.

A note on first principles

Cigar smoke and cigar smell are two distinct problems.

Most purifiers offer one remedy and assume it will suffice. Carbon-only units saturate against cigar VOC loads. Electrostatic precipitators surrender fine particles and emit ozone. Standalone ionisers ignore particulates altogether. Our specification provides both stages — properly engineered, properly sized, and proven together.

The order of works

One firm, from survey to commissioning.

A lounge is not a product specification but an environment, and an environment must perform on opening night and still perform five years on. We deliver projects in four phases, in the manner of any serious engineering practice, with a single point of accountability throughout.

i — Survey

Assess the space.

Room volume, ceiling height, existing ventilation capacity, projected smoking volume, the inventory of interior materials, the constraints of acoustics and aesthetics. We do not recommend equipment until we understand the room.

ii — Design

Engineer the system.

Particulate filtration, gas-phase ionisation, and pressure containment specified together. Unit selection, placement, BMS integration, acoustic targets — documented and signed off before a single device is dispatched.

iii — Integration

Coordinate the install.

Wall, ceiling, and floor units arrive ready to mount. In-duct modules are integrated by the property's HVAC contractor; we supply drawings, interface specifications, and direct coordination across Thailand and the wider region.

iv — Validation

Commission and prove it.

Airflow measured at every unit. Ionisation tube voltage and fan operation verified. BMS handshake confirmed. The maintenance schedule documented for the facilities team. The room is signed off only when it performs as designed.

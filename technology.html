Methodology

The science of clearing a room properly.

Three engineering pillars, each addressing a distinct failure mode. Mechanical filtration captures particulate. Bipolar plasma ionisation neutralises the gas-phase compounds that lend cigar its lingering scent. Negative-pressure containment ensures that both remain in the room to which they belong. Single-stage purifiers attempt one of these. Praan & Co. delivers all three, as a single specification.

i — The two pollutants

Cigar smoke is, simultaneously, a particulate aerosol and a chemical vapour.

Tobacco combustion produces hundreds of distinct compounds across two physical phases. The particulate phase is what one sees — micron and sub-micron droplets of tar, nicotine, and condensed combustion products suspended in air. Mechanical filters handle this category well.

The gas phase is what one smells. Pyridines, ammonia, hydrogen cyanide, formaldehyde, acrolein, phenols, aldehydes, ketones — volatile organic compounds (VOCs) which are individual molecules, far too small to be captured by any mechanical filter, regardless of MERV or HEPA rating.

The smoke clears when the air-handling does its work on the particulate phase. The scent persists because the gas phase remains suspended, continues to release from saturated soft furnishings, and lies beyond the reach of any filter mesh. This is why a room that appears clear may yet smell of cigar an hour, a day, or a week later.

ii — Stage one

Mechanical filtration: the proper means of handling particulates.

A staged filter train, simple, replaceable, and properly sized for the airflow.

Filter train · airflow direction →
Pre-filter Large particles, lint, ash
HEPA H13 99.97% at 0.3μm
Activated Carbon VOC adsorption layer
Plasma chamber Ion generation
Conditioned air Returned to room

Why this order matters.

The pre-filter protects the HEPA. The HEPA protects the carbon (loaded carbon adsorbs VOCs more efficiently when it is not also intercepting tar droplets). The carbon offers initial gas-phase adsorption. The plasma chamber follows, generating ions which are then carried into the room itself, where they continue to act upon VOCs that mechanical filters never encounter.

Activated carbon is genuinely effective at adsorbing many VOCs — but its capacity is finite. It saturates with use, and once it does, it ceases to adsorb. In cigar environments the load is heavy, and saturation arrives quickly. Carbon is part of the answer; it is not the whole answer.

iii — Stage two

Bipolar plasma ionisation: addressing odour at the molecular level.

Within the plasma chamber, a high-voltage transformer drives ionisation tubes which create a low-temperature plasma. Air molecules in the chamber are split into positive and negative ions — primarily oxygen and water-cluster ions — and these ions are carried out into the room by the airflow.

Once in the room, the ions interact with VOCs and microbial cells they encounter. The ion-VOC interaction breaks the molecular bonds of the odour compounds — pyridines, aldehydes, sulphur compounds — converting them into simpler, odourless molecules (water, carbon dioxide, and inert by-products). Microbes are inactivated by the same ion chemistry acting on cell membranes.

This is the difference between Praan & Co. and a sealed filter unit: ions act upon the air throughout the room, not solely upon the air that happens to pass through the device. They reach VOCs trapped in fabric, leather, drapery, and ceiling tiles — addressing the slow re-emission that causes cigar scent to linger for days after the cigar itself has finished.

iv — Containment

The third axis: keeping the smoke in the room to which it belongs.

A purifier, however well designed, acts only upon the air it draws through itself. The other half of the engineering brief is at the level of the room: how does one prevent cigar smoke and odour migrating into the corridor, the lobby, the adjoining suite, or the children's bedroom on the floor above?

Stable negative pressure.

A properly engineered cigar lounge or smoking room operates at a small but constant negative pressure relative to the surrounding spaces. Air flows inwards from adjacent rooms across door gaps and undercuts — never outwards. Smoke and odour have no pathway by which to migrate.

This is achieved by sizing the room's exhaust slightly higher than its supply, with the differential typically maintained between 5 and 15 Pascals. Done correctly, the lounge is invisible to the rest of the building. Done poorly, every guest who passes the front door knows precisely what lies within.

It is the single most common failure point in cigar-room HVAC retrofits, and the reason a perfectly good purifier within the lounge is not, on its own, sufficient.

Our delivery.

For new-build lounges, we work with the project's mechanical engineer on supply and exhaust balance from the outset. For retrofits, we typically install a dedicated exhaust path direct to outdoors, supplemented by in-duct PA-S ionisation modules on any return-air pathway which cannot be eliminated. In residential smoking rooms, the exhaust is sized to maintain negative pressure even when the door is open briefly during entry and exit.

The instrumentation is straightforward: a differential pressure sensor across the door, integrated into the BMS, with an alarm should pressure go positive. Once commissioned, the room remains contained automatically.

A note on electrostatic precipitators.

ESPs operate by charging particles and collecting them on oppositely charged plates. They are useful in certain industrial applications, but for occupied cigar environments they suffer from three persistent shortcomings:

First, fine and ultrafine particles slip through ESPs at higher rates than they do through HEPA media — precisely the size range cigar smoke produces in abundance. Second, the corona discharge that charges the particles also yields ozone as a by-product, a respiratory irritant and a regulatory concern in many jurisdictions. Third, ESPs do nothing for the gas phase, which means the scent persists even when the visible smoke has gone.

HEPA combined with activated carbon is the superior mechanical solution. Bipolar plasma addresses what mechanical filtration cannot. That is why Praan & Co. specifies both, as a matter of course.

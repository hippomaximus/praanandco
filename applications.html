Applications

Where the air has to be right.

Cigar lounges. Private smoking rooms. Hotel cigar bars. Retail humidors. Members' clubs. Yachts and private aviation. Each environment carries its own constraints, and its own definition of what an excellent atmosphere looks like for guests who have come to enjoy the smoke, not to eliminate it.

A different design brief

The aim is not a smoke-free room, but an excellent room with smoke in it.

Most commercial air purification is engineered to make smoke disappear, on the assumption that anyone in the room would prefer there to be none. Cigar environments invert that assumption entirely. The smoke is the point. What guests do not want is the haze that obscures the room, the headache attendant on accumulated particulates, the lingering odour that follows them home in their clothing, and the second-day staleness in upholstery and drapery.

Praan & Co. is engineered for that precise brief. We capture particulate. We neutralise the gas-phase compounds responsible for stale-smoke odour. We achieve both without diminishing the immediate aroma a guest is enjoying at their table. Below are the environments for which we are most often retained.

i · The Cigar Lounge

The flagship environment.

Dedicated cigar lounges are where the engineering earns its keep. Multiple cigars burning at once, ten to fifty guests, leather seating which absorbs odour as a sponge does water, and a clientele who notice immediately the difference between a lounge that has been thought about and one that has not.

What goes wrong without proper air-handling

Visible haze settles at eye level within an hour. Smoke detectors of the conventional sort begin to trigger false alarms. The leather, the drapery, and the ceiling acquire a permanent smell — one that takes weeks to recover after the lounge closes for renovation. Guests visiting for the first time encounter stale-smoke odour before they have taken their seats. Staff complain that their clothing and hair retain the scent long after a shift has ended.

Conventional HVAC alone cannot resolve this. One may ventilate at the rate that would keep a lounge clear, but the cost of conditioned makeup air becomes considerable — particularly in tropical climates such as Bangkok, Singapore, or Dubai, where outside air must be cooled and dehumidified before it can be introduced.

The Praan & Co. approach

A typical specification pairs ceiling-mounted recirculating units (PS-501T4 or PS-501T5 series) with one or two larger floor-standing units (PS-501T6A or PS-501TC) for active filtration during peak hours. In-duct ionisation tubes from the PA-S series are installed within the existing HVAC supply, ensuring that conditioned air entering the space arrives already ionised.

The result: visible smoke clears from sightlines within minutes, particulate settles on filter media rather than upon furniture, and the gas-phase compounds responsible for the "morning-after smell" are oxidised continuously rather than permitted to deposit. Walk into the lounge the next morning before staff arrive, and the room smells of a cigar lounge between sessions, not of an ashtray.

ii · The Private Smoking Room

A room one is reluctant to leave.

A dedicated smoking room in a residence, a private office, an executive suite, or a family compound. Smaller in volume than a commercial lounge, yet the air-quality stakes are arguably higher: this is where guests are entertained, where deals are concluded, and where the scent will travel into adjoining living spaces if it is not properly contained.

The residential challenge

Private smoking rooms typically range from 15 to 50 m² — well-finished, often with hardwood panelling, leather, or fabric that absorbs odour — and they share walls and ductwork with the rest of the residence. Even a modest quantity of cigar smoke migrating through return-air ducts or beneath doors will scent the master bedroom, the children's bedrooms, or the formal dining room. For most homeowners, this is the matter that prompts them to write.

The other constraint is acoustic. A loud industrial purifier destroys the atmosphere of a private room. Units specified for this category operate at low noise at low fan speeds, in wall-mount and ceiling-recessed forms that disappear into the architecture.

Our approach

For most private rooms a single PS-501TY wall-mount unit (up to 850 m³/h) or a PS-501T5 ceiling-recessed unit handles the active load silently. A negative-pressure strategy is then engineered: the smoking room is exhausted directly to outdoors via a dedicated low-pressure path, and an in-duct ionisation module on any return-air pathway prevents migration to adjacent rooms.

For homeowners who use the room only occasionally, we specify the PS-501T-A12 floor-standing unit — portable, plug-and-play, and capable of clearing the room rapidly between sessions and overnight.

iii · The Tobacconist & Humidor

Where customers come in to smell the tobacco, not yesterday's smoke.

Walk-in humidors and tobacconist retail floors carry a particular brief. The room must smell of fresh leaf, of cedar, and of the natural aroma of premium tobacco — not of the residual smoke of yesterday's customers sampling on the floor. And humidity, lighting, and temperature must remain locked at humidor specification whilst the air is being conditioned.

Why the humidor demands particular care

A walk-in humidor is engineered for stable temperature (typically 18–21°C) and stable relative humidity (65–72% RH). Aggressive air movement, ozone-generating purifiers, or excessive UV exposure each degrade the cigars in turn. Praan & Co.'s bipolar plasma technology is non-ozone-generating in normal operation; airflow on small humidor-specification units is gentle and adjustable; and the systems do not disturb humidity stability.

What they do affect is the airborne residue from staff and customers sampling on the retail floor — the origin of that "old shop" smell which makes a tobacconist feel tired rather than considered.

Our recommended specification

A wall-mounted PS-501TX or PS-501TR (according to floor area) on the retail side, paired with a small ceiling-recessed PS-501T1 within the humidor itself if it exceeds 8 to 10 m². The retail-floor unit runs continuously during opening hours; the humidor unit operates on a low-airflow setting calibrated to prevent odour compounds from depositing on inventory without disturbing humidity.

For boutique tobacconists with limited ceiling space, the wall-mounted PS-400T1 or PS-501TX is often the cleanest solution — small in stature, visually unobtrusive, and sufficiently quiet to run during customer interactions.

iv · Hospitality & Hotels

Smoking suites, hotel cigar bars, and the rooms adjacent.

Five-star hotels across Asia and the Middle East continue to operate designated smoking suites and entire smoking floors, and many maintain dedicated cigar bars or whisky lounges. The brand-protection challenge is unforgiving: a single guest complaint regarding smoke odour in a non-smoking room can cost the property a considerable booking premium for that floor.

The hotel-specific challenges

Hotel rooms turn over every 24 to 48 hours. Housekeeping has perhaps two hours to reset a smoking suite for a new guest who may be a non-smoker. The mattress, the drapery, the carpet, and the upholstered headboard each retain odour. Aerosol deodorisers mask the problem temporarily without resolving it — and many guests are sensitive enough to detect both the underlying smoke and the masking fragrance.

Beyond the room itself, the corridor outside, the lift lobby, and adjacent non-smoking rooms are all vulnerable to migration. Hotel mechanical systems were not, in most instances, designed for cigar-grade air loading.

The hospitality installation

For smoking suites: a single ceiling-recessed PS-501T5 with HEPA and activated carbon, running continuously, with operating mode automatically increased during housekeeping turnover so the room is reset before the next arrival. For hotel cigar bars: the standard cigar-lounge specification (above), scaled to the room volume.

For migration control: in-duct PA-S ionisation modules within the corridor return-air system on smoking floors, and stand-alone wall-mount units in lift lobbies adjacent to smoking-floor exits. The cumulative effect is that a non-smoking guest stepping out of the lift on a smoking floor does not immediately know they are on one.

v · The Whisky & Pairing Room

Where two aromatic worlds must coexist.

Whisky — particularly peated Scotch, fine bourbon, and aged rum — is an aromatic experience above all. Pair it with cigars, and one has one of the most demanding rooms in hospitality from an air-quality standpoint: guests must smell what is in the glass, not yesterday's smoke, and the cigars themselves cannot be muted by an over-aggressive air-handling system.

The pairing problem

Over-ventilate, and the cigar's volatile aromatic compounds are pulled from the immediate area before the smoker can enjoy the retrohale, while the whisky's nose fades against the air movement. Under-ventilate, and accumulated particulates and gas-phase compounds saturate the guest's olfactory receptors within twenty minutes — at which point the difference between a twelve-year and a twenty-five-year malt becomes academic.

The remedy

Systems specified for whisky lounges are tuned for low-velocity, high-volume recirculation. The bipolar plasma stage manages odour compounds without requiring aggressive airflow; the carbon stage holds VOCs at the molecular level; the HEPA stage manages particulate. The room remains aromatically alive — the cigar at the table reads cleanly, the whisky in the glass reads cleanly — and the cumulative drift toward smoke saturation that ruins long pairing menus is held in check.

vi · The Members' Club

Discretion, longevity, and no compromise.

Members' clubs — particularly those of multi-generational membership and considerable capital invested in their interiors — operate on a different time horizon to commercial venues. The leather is meant to age. The wood panelling is meant to last. The room itself is part of the asset. Air quality is, in this context, a question of preservation as much as one of comfort.

What clubs require

Members' clubs typically ask three things of an air-purification specification. First, that it be unobtrusive — ceiling-recessed, colour-matched, with no visible industrial equipment in member spaces. Second, that it prove reliable over a ten- to twenty-year operating horizon with predictable maintenance. Third, that it preserve the interiors themselves: the leather, the silk wall coverings, the original panelling, the rare volumes in the library wing.

Our delivery

Stainless-steel finish PS-501T5A or PS-501T5B ceiling units with optional fresh-air integration, paired with in-duct PA-S ionisation modules operating continuously within the building's HVAC return. For libraries and reading rooms adjacent to the smoking room, dedicated PS-501TR wall units with high-efficiency filtration prevent any cross-room migration that might affect rare materials. Filter and tube replacement schedules are documented, predictable, and serviced by appointed dealers.

Installation & ongoing service

How a project is run.

Site survey and specification

Before any equipment is recommended, we measure or request the room volume, ceiling height, existing HVAC capacity, smoking-volume estimate (cigars per evening, peak occupancy), inventory of interior materials, and noise constraints. From these particulars we propose a unit selection and placement plan that meets the brief without over- or under-specifying.

Delivery and installation

Wall-mount, ceiling-recessed, and floor-standing units are delivered ready to install. In-duct PA-S ionisation modules are integrated by a qualified HVAC contractor; we supply the integration drawings and the BMS interface specification. For larger projects across Thailand and Southeast Asia we coordinate directly with the property's mechanical contractor.

Commissioning

On commissioning, we verify airflow at the unit, ionisation tube voltage and fan operation, and the operation of any control panel or BMS integration. We document the maintenance schedule for the property's facilities team, and provide replacement-part identification for the carbon, HEPA, and ionisation tube components.

Ongoing service

Plasma tubes are rated to 17,000 hours — approximately two years of continuous operation. Pre-filters are washable. HEPA and activated carbon are replaced on a schedule which depends on smoking load. We supply genuine replacement parts, and recommend bi-annual inspection for commercial installations.
